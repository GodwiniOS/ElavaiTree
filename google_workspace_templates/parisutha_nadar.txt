A. Y. S. Parisutha Nadar

A. Y. S. Parisutha Nadar (13 April 1909 – 14 May 1985) was a prominent Indian politician, philanthropist, and community leader from Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu. He was widely respected for his social service, developmental contributions to Thanjavur, and close association with Congress leader K. Kamaraj.

Early Life & Family Background
Parisutha Nadar was born into a Roman Catholic Nadar family on April 13, 1909, in Thanjavur. He was the youngest of three siblings born to A. Yagappa Nadar (a community elder and merchant) and Gnanammal. His elder brother was A. Y. Arulanthasamy Nadar and his sister was Idhayameri Ammal. 

Education
He received his primary and secondary schooling at St. Anthony’s High School in Thanjavur and pursued his higher studies at the prestigious St. Joseph’s College in Tiruchirappalli, where he stood out as an active student leader.

Marriage & Children
He was married to Philomena Soosaiammal. Together they raised their family in Thanjavur, carrying forward a legacy of philanthropy and business. His children include S. P. Selvaraj (Managing Director of Hotel Gnanam) and S. P. Anthonisamy (Chairman of the Parisutham Institute of Technology and Science).

Career & Political Life
Parisutha Nadar was an active member of the Indian National Congress and held significant influence in regional politics for several decades:
- Legislative Assembly: He served as the Member of the Legislative Assembly (MLA) for the Thanjavur constituency, winning elections in 1946 (Madras Presidency), 1957, and 1967.
- Municipal Chairman: He was the Chairman of the Thanjavur Municipality from 1959 to 1961, implementing major urban upgrades.
- Congress Party Leadership: He served as the Thanjavur Town Congress President for 21 consecutive years and was a member of both the Tamil Nadu Congress Committee (TNCC) and the All India Congress Committee (AICC).
- World War II Service: During World War II, he served as an Air Raid Warden. In recognition of his public services, the government conferred upon him the title of "Rao Saheb".

Philanthropy & Infrastructural Legacy
He was instrumental in establishing major institutions in Thanjavur:
- Thanjavur Medical College: With the assistance of Chief Minister K. Kamaraj, he spearheaded the establishment of the medical college. His family donated prime lands for the creation of the Rajah Serfoji Government College.
- Public Health & Utilities: He successfully implemented the comprehensive Thirumanur drinking water scheme, securing clean water pipeline distribution for the Thanjavur municipality.
- Civic Organizations: He gave strong financial support to the Guild of Service and the Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (SPCA).

Aviation & Sports Interests
Parisutha Nadar was a versatile sportsman. He played football for the Madras State team and excelled in billiards (securing second place at the state championship) and tennis. Remarkably, at the age of 61, he obtained a pilot's license and completed solo flights, demonstrating his lifelong passion for adventure.

Centenary & Memorials
To continue his charitable contributions, the A. Y. S. Parisutha Nadar Centenary Foundation was established by his descendants. The foundation manages the Gnanam School of Business, which hosts annual memorial lectures and awards in his honor.
