A. Yagappa Nadar

A. Yagappa Nadar (15 August 1880 – 20 March 1955) was a highly respected merchant, community leader, and prominent philanthropist in Thanjavur, South India. He is widely recognized for his civic service and for establishing a legacy of social and educational advancement.

Early Life & Commerce
Yagappa Nadar was born in August 1880 in the Tanjore region. He established successful mercantile trading networks during the Madras Presidency. Recognizing the power of education to uplift the community, he prioritized sending his children to prestigious academic institutions.

Philanthropic Legacy
Yagappa Nadar was deeply committed to community welfare. His family donated extensive tracts of prime land that made the establishment of the Thanjavur Medical College and Rajah Serfoji Government College possible. His contributions laid the foundation for modern higher education and healthcare in Thanjavur district.

Family
He was married to Gnanammal Nadar. They raised three children: their daughter Idhayameri Ammal, and two sons, A. Y. Arulanthasamy Nadar and A. Y. S. Parisutha Nadar.
